Federal Grants for Disability Services and Accessibility

Organizations serving people with disabilities can access federal funding for independent living services, assistive technology, employment programs, accessible housing, and community integration. The Administration for Community Living (ACL), Social Security Administration, Department of Labor, and other agencies invest in programs promoting independence, inclusion, and self-determination.

These grants support Centers for Independent Living, assistive technology programs, supported employment, and accessibility improvements. Staying informed about funding opportunities helps you expand services and remove barriers.

GrantPing helps disability services organizations track federal funding.

Major Federal Disability Services Grant Programs

1. ACL Independent Living Services for Older Individuals Who Are Blind

The OIB program provides independent living services to older individuals who are blind or visually impaired. Awards typically range from $100,000 to $400,000 per state, distributed to local service providers.

2. ACL State Councils on Developmental Disabilities (DD Councils)

DD Councils receive formula funding to support systems change, capacity building, and advocacy for people with developmental disabilities. Councils fund local projects ranging from $25,000 to $200,000.

3. ACL Centers for Independent Living (CILs)

CIL formula and competitive grants support consumer-controlled organizations providing independent living services. Awards range from $100,000 to $400,000 annually.

4. SSA Ticket to Work Program

The Social Security Administration funds Employment Networks providing job placement and support services to Social Security disability beneficiaries. Payment is based on outcomes.

5. DOL Disability Employment Initiative

The Department of Labor funds workforce development systems to improve employment outcomes for people with disabilities. Awards typically range from $500,000 to $2 million.

Recent Opportunities

ACL Assistive Technology State Grant Program

Agency: Administration for Community Living

Typical Award: $400,000 - $600,000 per state

Summary: Formula grants to states supporting assistive technology device demonstration, loan programs, reutilization, and financing. States distribute funding to organizations providing AT access, information, and training.

ACL National Institute on Disability, Independent Living, and Rehabilitation Research (NIDILRR)

Agency: Administration for Community Living

Typical Award: $450,000 - $750,000/year for 5 years

Summary: Funds research and development projects advancing knowledge and practice in disability, independent living, and rehabilitation. Supports research centers, field-initiated projects, and development projects.

DOL Disability Employment Initiative Grants

Agency: Department of Labor

Typical Award: $750,000 - $2,000,000

Summary: Supports state workforce systems in improving employment outcomes for people with disabilities through customized employment, benefits counseling, assistive technology, and employer engagement strategies.

Who Is Eligible?

Disability services grant eligibility typically includes:

  • Centers for Independent Living (CILs)
  • 501(c)(3) disability rights and services organizations
  • State assistive technology programs
  • State developmental disabilities councils
  • State vocational rehabilitation agencies
  • Universities with disability research programs
  • Employment Networks (for SSA programs)
  • Community rehabilitation providers

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful disability services grants typically support:

  • Independent living services - Peer counseling, skills training, advocacy, information & referral
  • Assistive technology - Device loans, demonstrations, financing, reutilization programs
  • Employment services - Supported employment, customized employment, job coaching
  • Accessibility improvements - Home modifications, communication access, transportation
  • Systems advocacy - Policy change, community education, rights enforcement
  • Youth transition - School-to-work programs, post-secondary education support
  • Benefits counseling - Work incentives planning, SSI/SSDI navigation
  • Research and development - Innovative assistive technologies, service delivery models
