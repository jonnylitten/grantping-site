USDA and Federal Grants for Rural Development

Rural communities face unique challenges, from aging infrastructure to limited broadband access. The U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) and other federal agencies invest billions annually in rural development grants supporting infrastructure, economic development, healthcare, and community facilities.

Rural development grants can transform communities, but finding and applying for the right opportunities requires staying current with funding announcements and understanding complex eligibility requirements.

GrantPing helps rural communities and nonprofits track federal funding opportunities.

Major Federal Rural Development Grant Programs

1. USDA Community Facilities Direct Loan and Grant Program

USDA Rural Development provides grants and loans to develop essential community facilities including healthcare clinics, fire stations, schools, libraries, and community centers in rural areas. Grants can cover up to 75% of project costs, with awards ranging from $50,000 to $2 million.

2. USDA ReConnect Program (Rural Broadband)

The ReConnect Program funds broadband infrastructure in underserved rural areas. Awards include 100% grants up to $25 million, loan/grant combinations up to $50 million, and loans up to $100 million.

3. USDA Rural Business Development Grants

This program supports rural business development through training, technical assistance, and planning activities. Awards range from $10,000 to $500,000 for nonprofit organizations serving rural businesses.

4. USDA Distance Learning and Telemedicine Grants

Provides funding for telecommunications equipment and technology for rural schools, libraries, and healthcare facilities. Awards range from $50,000 to $1 million.

5. Appalachian Regional Commission Grants

The ARC funds infrastructure, economic development, and community capacity building in the 13-state Appalachian region. Awards typically range from $100,000 to $500,000.

Agency: U.S. Department of Agriculture

Typical Award: $100,000 - $2,000,000

Summary: Supports construction, enlargement, or improvement of rural health clinics, hospitals, dental facilities, and emergency medical facilities serving populations of 20,000 or fewer.

Agency: Delta Regional Authority

Typical Award: $100,000 - $750,000

Summary: Funds basic infrastructure projects in transportation, water/wastewater systems, workforce training facilities, and community centers in the Mississippi Delta region.

Who Is Eligible?

Rural development grant eligibility varies by program but typically includes:

  • Rural municipalities and counties (populations under 20,000)
  • 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations serving rural areas
  • Tribal governments
  • Rural electric cooperatives and utilities
  • State and local governments
  • Educational institutions serving rural communities

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful rural development grants typically support:

  • Broadband infrastructure - Fiber-optic networks, wireless systems
  • Community facilities - Fire stations, community centers, libraries
  • Healthcare facilities - Rural health clinics, telemedicine equipment
  • Water and wastewater systems - Treatment plants, distribution systems
  • Economic development - Business incubators, technical assistance
  • Renewable energy - Solar, wind, biomass projects
  • Educational facilities - Distance learning technology, training centers
