CPB Grants for Public Television and Radio

Public television and radio stations receive federal funding through the Corporation for Public Broadcasting (CPB), which distributes grants for operations, programming, technology, and community service. These grants support high-quality educational content, local journalism, and community engagement.

CPB grants are essential to public broadcasting sustainability. Staying informed about funding opportunities, qualification requirements, and special initiatives helps stations maximize federal support.

Major Federal Public Broadcasting Grant Programs

1. CPB Community Service Grants (CSG)

CSG is the primary federal funding source for public broadcasters. Grants support station operations, programming, and community service. Television CSG awards range from $100,000 to over $2 million; radio CSG awards range from $50,000 to $400,000, based on station audience and revenue.

2. CPB Public Media Modernization and Infrastructure Grants

These infrastructure grants support technology upgrades, transmitter replacement, and facility improvements. Awards typically range from $100,000 to $1 million for equipment and capital projects.

3. CPB Next Generation Warning System (NGWS)

The NGWS program funds public broadcasters to serve as emergency alert originators during disasters. Awards support equipment and operational costs for stations participating in emergency communications.

4. CPB American Graduate: Getting to Work

American Graduate grants support educational programming and community initiatives addressing high school graduation, college completion, and workforce readiness. Awards range from $25,000 to $200,000.

5. CPB Local Journalism Initiative

This program funds local journalism projects at public media stations, supporting community news, investigative reporting, and information services. Awards range from $50,000 to $300,000.

Recent Opportunities

CPB Community Service Grant - Television

Agency: Corporation for Public Broadcasting

Typical Award: $200,000 - $2,500,000

Summary: Annual formula grants to qualified public television stations based on Non-Federal Financial Support (NFFS). Funds operations, programming acquisition, local content production, and community engagement. Stations must meet qualification criteria including broadcast hours, management standards, and financial reporting.

CPB Community Service Grant - Radio

Agency: Corporation for Public Broadcasting

Typical Award: $75,000 - $500,000

Summary: Annual formula grants to qualified public radio stations. Award amount based on station NFFS, with minimum grants for small and rural stations. Supports operations, national program acquisition, local production, and digital services.

CPB Public Safety and Homeland Security Interconnection Grants

Agency: Corporation for Public Broadcasting

Typical Award: $50,000 - $400,000

Summary: Supports public broadcasters in establishing interconnection with public safety networks for emergency alerting and information distribution during disasters. Funds equipment, training, and coordination with emergency management agencies.

Who Is Eligible?

Public broadcasting grant eligibility typically includes:

  • CPB-qualified public television stations
  • CPB-qualified public radio stations
  • Public broadcasting entities (state networks, multi-station licensees)
  • Tribal public broadcasting stations
  • Educational institutions operating public broadcasting
  • Community organizations with broadcast licenses

Stations must meet CPB qualification criteria including minimum broadcast hours, financial standards, and community service requirements.

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful public broadcasting grants typically support:

  • Station operations - Salaries, programming costs, utilities, overhead
  • National programming - PBS and NPR content licensing
  • Local content production - News, documentaries, community affairs
  • Technology upgrades - Transmitter replacement, studio equipment, digital infrastructure
  • Emergency preparedness - Emergency alerting systems, backup power
  • Educational initiatives - STEM programming, literacy campaigns, adult education
  • Community engagement - Outreach, partnerships, audience services
  • Journalism - Investigative reporting, civic engagement, local news
