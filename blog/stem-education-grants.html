Federal Grants for STEM Education Programs

STEM education is a federal funding priority. Whether you run an afterschool robotics program, a summer coding camp, or a K-12 science initiative, billions of dollars in federal grants support STEM learning each year.

The challenge? Finding the right opportunities and meeting tight deadlines. STEM grants are highly competitive, and organizations that don't monitor opportunities daily often miss their chance.

GrantPing helps STEM educators and nonprofits stay on top of federal funding opportunities.

Major Federal STEM Grant Programs

1. NSF Innovative Technology Experiences for Students and Teachers (ITEST)

The National Science Foundation ITEST program funds projects that prepare K-12 students and teachers for careers in STEM fields. Awards typically range from $500,000 to $1.5 million over 3 years.

2. Department of Education STEM Discretionary Grants

The U.S. Department of Education offers competitive grants including the Supporting Effective Educator Development (SEED) program and Education Innovation and Research (EIR) grants. Awards range from $500,000 to $4 million.

3. NASA STEM Education and Accountability Projects

NASA funds education programs that engage students in authentic STEM experiences. Programs include the Minority University Research and Education Project (MUREP) and Space Grant Consortium. Awards range from $100,000 to $750,000.

4. 21st Century Community Learning Centers (21st CCLC)

This Department of Education program provides funding for afterschool and summer enrichment programs with strong STEM components. State-administered grants typically award $50,000 to $400,000 per year to local organizations.

5. NOAA Bay Watershed Education and Training (B-WET)

NOAA supports environmental STEM education programs focused on coastal and watershed science. Awards typically range from $50,000 to $200,000.

Recent Opportunities

NSF Advanced Technological Education (ATE) Program

Agency: National Science Foundation

Typical Award: $200,000 - $3,000,000

Summary: Supports technician education in high-technology fields including cybersecurity, biotechnology, advanced manufacturing, and clean energy. Funds community college programs and K-12 pathways.

Education Innovation and Research (EIR) Grants

Agency: U.S. Department of Education

Typical Award: $500,000 - $4,000,000

Summary: Funds innovative STEM education approaches with evidence of effectiveness. Supports projects serving low-income students and underrepresented groups in STEM fields.

NASA Minority University Research and Education Project (MUREP)

Agency: National Aeronautics and Space Administration

Typical Award: $150,000 - $600,000

Summary: Supports STEM engagement at Minority Serving Institutions and provides student internships, curriculum development, and research opportunities in aerospace fields.

Who Is Eligible?

STEM grant eligibility varies by program but typically includes:

  • K-12 school districts and individual schools
  • 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations
  • Community colleges and universities
  • Informal education organizations (science museums, libraries)
  • State and local education agencies
  • Tribal education departments

Many programs encourage or require partnerships between schools, nonprofits, universities, and industry partners.

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Competitive STEM education grants typically support:

  • Hands-on learning programs - Robotics clubs, coding bootcamps, maker spaces
  • Teacher professional development - STEM training and curriculum support
  • Underrepresented student recruitment - Programs for girls, minorities, and rural students
  • Career pathway programs - Internships, mentorships, industry partnerships
  • Curriculum innovation - Project-based learning, interdisciplinary approaches
  • Technology integration - Computational thinking, data science, engineering design
