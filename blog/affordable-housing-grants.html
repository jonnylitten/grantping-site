HUD Grants for Affordable Housing Development

Affordable housing developers, community development corporations, housing authorities, and nonprofits can access billions in federal funding from the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) and other agencies. These grants support new construction, rehabilitation, homeownership programs, rental assistance, and supportive housing.

Affordable housing grants are highly competitive and often require matching funds, development experience, and complex compliance. Staying informed about funding opportunities is essential to creating housing for low-income families and individuals.

GrantPing helps affordable housing developers track federal funding opportunities.

Major Federal Affordable Housing Grant Programs

1. HUD HOME Investment Partnerships Program

The HOME program provides formula grants to states and localities for affordable housing development, rehabilitation, and tenant-based rental assistance. Local jurisdictions distribute funds to developers. Typical project awards range from $500,000 to $5 million.

2. HUD Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) - Housing Activities

CDBG funds can support housing rehabilitation, homeownership assistance, and affordable housing development. Entitlement communities and states allocate CDBG funding to housing projects.

3. Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC)

While not a grant, LIHTC is the primary federal tool for affordable rental housing development. State housing finance agencies allocate tax credits to developers. A typical 9% LIHTC project can generate $10-20 million in equity.

4. HUD Housing Trust Fund (HTF)

The HTF provides funding for extremely low-income housing development. Formula grants to states support new construction and rehabilitation. Typical project awards range from $750,000 to $3 million.

5. HUD Housing Counseling Grants

These grants support nonprofit housing counseling agencies providing homeownership, rental, foreclosure prevention, and homeless counseling. Awards range from $50,000 to $500,000.

Recent Opportunities

HUD HOME Investment Partnerships Program

Agency: U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development

Typical Award: $750,000 - $5,000,000 (via state/local agencies)

Summary: Formula grants to states and localities for affordable housing production and preservation. Funds new construction, rehabilitation, acquisition, tenant-based rental assistance, and homebuyer assistance. Developers apply to local Participating Jurisdictions.

HUD Housing Opportunities for Persons with AIDS (HOPWA)

Agency: U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development

Typical Award: $250,000 - $2,000,000

Summary: Formula and competitive grants for housing assistance and supportive services for low-income people living with HIV/AIDS. Supports rental assistance, short-term housing payments, and permanent housing development.

HUD Section 811 Supportive Housing for Persons with Disabilities

Agency: U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development

Typical Award: $1,000,000 - $5,000,000

Summary: Capital advances and project rental assistance for supportive housing serving extremely low-income adults with disabilities. Funds new construction, rehabilitation, and acquisition with long-term rental subsidies.

Who Is Eligible?

Affordable housing grant eligibility typically includes:

  • Community Housing Development Organizations (CHDOs)
  • 501(c)(3) nonprofit housing developers
  • Public housing authorities
  • For-profit affordable housing developers (for some programs)
  • State and local governments
  • Community development corporations
  • Tribal housing authorities

Many programs require demonstrated development capacity and financial strength.

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful affordable housing grants typically support:

  • New construction - Affordable rental apartments, single-family homes
  • Rehabilitation - Historic preservation, vacant building conversion
  • Homeownership programs - Downpayment assistance, homebuyer counseling
  • Permanent supportive housing - Housing with services for homeless, disabled
  • Senior housing - Independent living, assisted living facilities
  • Rental assistance - Tenant-based vouchers, project-based subsidies
  • Mixed-income developments - Revitalization of distressed neighborhoods
  • Acquisition and preservation - Preserving existing affordable housing
