DOL Grants for Job Training and Workforce Development

Workforce development organizations, community colleges, and nonprofits providing job training can access billions in federal funding from the U.S. Department of Labor (DOL) and other agencies. These grants support apprenticeships, skills training, career pathways, and employment services for jobseekers.

Workforce grants are highly competitive and often target specific populations or industries. Staying current with funding announcements is essential to securing support for your programs.

GrantPing helps workforce development providers track federal funding opportunities.

Major Federal Workforce Development Grant Programs

1. DOL Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) Grants

The WIOA is the primary federal workforce development program, distributing formula grants to states and competitive grants for special initiatives. Competitive grants range from $500,000 to $5 million for sector partnerships, apprenticeships, and innovative training models.

2. DOL Apprenticeship Building America Grants

The Department of Labor funds expansion of Registered Apprenticeship programs, including pre-apprenticeships and youth apprenticeships. Awards typically range from $500,000 to $2 million.

3. DOL H-1B Job Training Grants

Funded through H-1B visa fees, these competitive grants support training for in-demand industries including technology, healthcare, and advanced manufacturing. Awards range from $1 million to $5 million.

4. EDA Good Jobs Challenge

The Economic Development Administration funds workforce training systems that connect workers to quality jobs in growth industries. Awards range from $5 million to $50 million for regional partnerships.

5. DOL Trade Adjustment Assistance Community College and Career Training (TAACCCT)

While the original program has concluded, similar DOL initiatives continue funding community college training programs in high-demand fields. Awards typically range from $2 million to $5 million.

Recent Opportunities

DOL Workforce Pathways for Youth

Agency: Department of Labor, Employment and Training Administration

Typical Award: $1,000,000 - $3,500,000

Summary: Supports partnerships developing career pathways for opportunity youth ages 16-24, including work-based learning, industry-recognized credentials, and wraparound supportive services.

DOL Strengthening Community Colleges Training Grants

Agency: Department of Labor

Typical Award: $2,000,000 - $4,000,000

Summary: Funds community colleges to develop and expand training programs in high-growth sectors including clean energy, advanced manufacturing, healthcare, and information technology.

DOL Reentry Employment Opportunities (REO)

Agency: Department of Labor

Typical Award: $750,000 - $2,000,000

Summary: Supports employment and training services for formerly incarcerated individuals, including job placement, occupational skills training, and employer engagement strategies.

Who Is Eligible?

Workforce development grant eligibility typically includes:

  • Community colleges and technical schools
  • 501(c)(3) nonprofit workforce organizations
  • State and local workforce development boards
  • Industry partnerships and sector collaboratives
  • Labor unions and joint labor-management organizations
  • Economic development organizations
  • Educational institutions

Many programs require or strongly prefer partnerships between training providers, employers, and workforce boards.

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful workforce development grants typically support:

  • Registered apprenticeships - Earn-and-learn models in skilled trades and emerging fields
  • Sector partnerships - Industry-led training addressing employer needs
  • Career pathways - Stackable credentials leading to family-sustaining wages
  • Incumbent worker training - Upskilling current employees
  • Services for special populations - Youth, older workers, justice-involved, people with disabilities
  • Work-based learning - Internships, on-the-job training, pre-apprenticeships
  • Industry-recognized credentials - Certification programs aligned with labor market demand
