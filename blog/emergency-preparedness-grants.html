FEMA Grants for Emergency Preparedness and Disaster Resilience

State and local emergency management agencies, nonprofits, and communities can access federal funding from FEMA, HUD, NOAA, and other agencies for emergency preparedness, disaster mitigation, climate resilience, and recovery planning. These grants support everything from emergency operations centers to flood mitigation projects, community warning systems to evacuation planning.

As climate change increases disaster frequency and severity, federal investment in preparedness and resilience continues to grow. Staying informed about funding opportunities helps communities reduce risk and build capacity to respond to emergencies.

Major Federal Emergency Preparedness Grant Programs

1. FEMA Building Resilient Infrastructure and Communities (BRIC)

The BRIC program funds hazard mitigation projects that reduce disaster risks. Awards range from $1 million to $50 million for infrastructure improvements, nature-based solutions, and community resilience planning.

2. FEMA Emergency Management Performance Grant (EMPG)

EMPG provides formula funding to state and local emergency management agencies for planning, training, exercises, and operations. Awards support emergency operations centers, warning systems, and staff salaries.

3. FEMA Flood Mitigation Assistance (FMA)

The FMA program funds projects reducing flood risk in National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) communities. Awards range from $500,000 to $10 million for property acquisition, elevation, and floodplain restoration.

4. HUD Community Development Block Grant - Disaster Recovery (CDBG-DR)

CDBG-DR provides flexible funding for long-term disaster recovery in presidentially-declared disasters. Awards to states and localities range from $10 million to billions for housing, infrastructure, and economic revitalization.

5. NOAA Climate Resilience Regional Challenge

NOAA funds collaborative approaches to coastal resilience and climate adaptation. Awards range from $1 million to $50 million for habitat restoration, green infrastructure, and community resilience.

Recent Opportunities

FEMA Building Resilient Infrastructure and Communities (BRIC)

Agency: Federal Emergency Management Agency

Typical Award: $1,000,000 - $50,000,000

Summary: Competitive grants for hazard mitigation projects reducing risk from natural disasters. Funds infrastructure improvements, property acquisition, retrofits, nature-based solutions, and capability- and capacity-building activities. Supports climate adaptation and community resilience.

FEMA Pre-Disaster Mitigation and Hazard Mitigation Grant Program

Agency: Federal Emergency Management Agency

Typical Award: $500,000 - $20,000,000

Summary: Funds mitigation projects before and after disasters including safe rooms, property acquisition, structural elevation, drainage improvements, and wildfire mitigation. Reduces long-term risk to life and property from natural hazards.

FEMA Nonprofit Security Grant Program (NSGP)

Agency: Federal Emergency Management Agency

Typical Award: $75,000 - $150,000

Summary: Supports target-hardening and security enhancements for nonprofit organizations at high risk of terrorist attack. Funds physical security improvements, surveillance systems, access control, security training, and planning.

Who Is Eligible?

Emergency preparedness grant eligibility typically includes:

  • State and local emergency management agencies
  • State and local governments
  • Tribal governments
  • 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations
  • Regional planning commissions
  • Public utilities and special districts
  • Educational institutions

Many programs require applicants to have FEMA-approved hazard mitigation plans or participate in the National Flood Insurance Program.

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful emergency preparedness grants typically support:

  • Emergency operations centers - Construction, renovation, equipment, technology
  • Flood mitigation - Property acquisition, elevation, drainage improvements
  • Hazard mitigation planning - Risk assessments, mitigation strategies, community engagement
  • Warning systems - Sirens, emergency alerts, public notification infrastructure
  • Training and exercises - Tabletop exercises, full-scale drills, responder training
  • Climate resilience - Green infrastructure, coastal protection, heat mitigation
  • Community safe rooms - Tornado shelters, hurricane shelters
  • Disaster recovery planning - Long-term recovery frameworks, housing strategies
