National Park Service Grants for Historic Preservation

Historic preservation organizations, municipalities, tribal governments, and nonprofits can access federal funding from the National Park Service (NPS), National Endowment for the Humanities (NEH), and other agencies. These grants support restoration of historic buildings, archaeology projects, heritage education, and preservation planning.

Historic preservation grants protect America's cultural heritage while supporting economic development through heritage tourism. Staying informed about funding opportunities helps preserve significant historic resources.

GrantPing helps historic preservation organizations track federal funding.

Major Federal Historic Preservation Grant Programs

1. NPS Historic Preservation Fund (HPF) Grants

The HPF provides formula grants to State Historic Preservation Offices (SHPOs), which then distribute funds to local preservation projects. Subgrants typically range from $5,000 to $100,000 for surveys, National Register nominations, and preservation planning.

2. NPS Save America's Treasures

Save America's Treasures funds preservation and conservation of nationally significant historic properties and collections. Competitive awards range from $75,000 to $750,000 with required 1:1 match.

3. NPS African American Civil Rights Grant Program

This program funds preservation of sites related to the African American Civil Rights movement (1939-1968). Awards range from $50,000 to $500,000 for restoration, interpretation, and education projects.

4. NPS Underrepresented Community Grants

These grants support preservation of sites associated with underrepresented communities including women, LGBTQ, Asian Americans, and Latino Americans. Awards range from $50,000 to $500,000.

5. NEH Preservation and Access Grants

The National Endowment for the Humanities funds preservation of humanities collections including historic documents, photographs, and architectural records. Awards range from $5,000 to $400,000.

Recent Opportunities

NPS Save America's Treasures Grants

Agency: National Park Service

Typical Award: $75,000 - $750,000

Summary: Competitive grants for preservation and conservation of nationally significant intellectual and cultural artifacts and historic structures. Properties must be designated National Historic Landmarks or listed in National Register. Requires 1:1 cash match.

NPS African American Civil Rights Grant Program

Agency: National Park Service

Typical Award: $100,000 - $500,000

Summary: Funds preservation of historic sites and structures significant to African American Civil Rights history (1939-1968). Supports restoration, rehabilitation, interpretation, and educational programming. Properties must be listed or eligible for National Register.

NPS Historic Preservation Fund - Tribal Heritage Grants

Agency: National Park Service

Typical Award: $25,000 - $150,000

Summary: Formula grants to Tribal Historic Preservation Offices (THPOs) for surveys, National Register nominations, preservation planning, and heritage education on tribal lands. Supports documentation of traditional cultural properties and archaeological sites.

Who Is Eligible?

Historic preservation grant eligibility typically includes:

  • State and Tribal Historic Preservation Offices
  • 501(c)(3) nonprofit preservation organizations
  • Local governments and municipalities
  • Museums and historical societies
  • Educational institutions
  • Historic site owners (public and private)
  • Certified Local Governments (CLGs)

Most programs require properties to be listed or eligible for listing on the National Register of Historic Places.

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful historic preservation grants typically support:

  • Building restoration - Exterior rehabilitation, structural stabilization, historic materials conservation
  • Archaeology projects - Excavation, analysis, site preservation
  • Historic structure reports - Architectural documentation, condition assessments
  • National Register nominations - Research, documentation, listing
  • Preservation planning - Surveys, historic context studies, management plans
  • Heritage education - Interpretive programs, exhibits, public programming
  • Collections preservation - Archival materials, photographs, documents
  • Traditional cultural properties - Documentation and protection of sacred sites
