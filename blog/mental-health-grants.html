SAMHSA and Federal Mental Health Service Grants

Mental health and behavioral health organizations provide critical services to individuals experiencing mental illness, emotional disturbances, and co-occurring disorders. Federal grants from the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA), HRSA, and other HHS agencies fund treatment programs, crisis services, prevention initiatives, and workforce development.

These grants support everything from community mental health centers to school-based counseling, crisis intervention teams to peer support programs. Staying informed about funding opportunities is essential to sustaining and expanding mental health services.

Major Federal Mental Health Grant Programs

1. SAMHSA Community Mental Health Services Block Grant

The Mental Health Block Grant provides formula funding to states and territories for community-based mental health services. States then distribute funding to local providers. Typical subawards range from $100,000 to $1 million.

2. SAMHSA Project AWARE (Advancing Wellness and Resilience in Education)

Project AWARE funds states to increase awareness of mental health issues among school-aged youth and connect them to services. Awards typically range from $2 million to $8 million over 5 years.

3. SAMHSA Certified Community Behavioral Health Clinics (CCBHC)

The CCBHC program supports comprehensive mental health and substance use treatment services through certified community behavioral health clinics. Expansion grants range from $2 million to $4 million annually.

4. HRSA Behavioral Health Workforce Education and Training (BHWET)

BHWET funds universities and training programs to prepare behavioral health professionals including social workers, psychologists, counselors, and peer specialists. Awards range from $500,000 to $2 million over 5 years.

5. SAMHSA National Child Traumatic Stress Initiative

This program supports trauma-informed mental health services for children and adolescents. Awards typically range from $300,000 to $500,000 annually.

Recent Opportunities

SAMHSA Community Mental Health Services Block Grant

Agency: Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration

Typical Award: $200,000 - $1,500,000 (via state agencies)

Summary: Formula grants to states supporting comprehensive community mental health services for adults with serious mental illness and children with serious emotional disturbances. Local providers apply through state mental health authorities.

SAMHSA Mental Health Crisis Response Partnerships

Agency: Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration

Typical Award: $500,000 - $2,000,000

Summary: Supports partnerships between mental health providers and law enforcement to develop crisis response teams, mobile crisis units, and crisis stabilization centers as alternatives to incarceration and emergency departments.

SAMHSA Youth Mental Health and Substance Use Disorder Treatment Grant

Agency: Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration

Typical Award: $400,000 - $900,000/year

Summary: Funds evidence-based mental health and substance use treatment services for youth and young adults with serious mental health conditions or substance use disorders, with focus on underserved populations.

Who Is Eligible?

Mental health grant eligibility typically includes:

  • Community mental health centers
  • 501(c)(3) behavioral health organizations
  • State and local mental health authorities
  • Hospitals with psychiatric departments
  • School districts and educational institutions
  • Universities with behavioral health training programs
  • Tribal behavioral health programs

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful mental health grants typically support:

  • Outpatient mental health treatment - Individual, group, and family therapy
  • Crisis intervention services - Mobile crisis teams, crisis stabilization units
  • Peer support programs - Peer specialists, recovery support services
  • School-based mental health - Counseling, screening, referral services
  • Integrated care models - Co-locating mental health in primary care settings
  • Workforce development - Training, supervision, loan repayment for providers
  • Prevention and early intervention - Screening, education, resilience building
  • Trauma-informed care - Services for trauma survivors, ACEs interventions
