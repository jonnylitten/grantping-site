Major Federal Grant Programs for Environmental Nonprofits

Environmental nonprofits can access funding from multiple federal agencies. Here are the most significant programs:

1. EPA Environmental Education Grants

The Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) offers competitive grants to support environmental education projects that promote environmental awareness and stewardship. Typical awards range from $50,000 to $100,000.

2. NOAA Coastal and Marine Habitat Restoration Grants

The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) provides funding through its Community-based Restoration Program and other initiatives. Projects include wetland restoration, oyster reef rebuilding, and coral reef conservation. Grants typically range from $100,000 to $500,000.

3. USDA Natural Resources Conservation Service (NRCS) Programs

The USDA NRCS offers conservation grants including the Regional Conservation Partnership Program (RCPP) and the Conservation Innovation Grants (CIG). These support sustainable agriculture, water quality improvement, and wildlife habitat conservation. Awards can exceed $1 million for collaborative projects.

4. National Fish and Wildlife Foundation (NFWF) Grants

While not directly federal, NFWF distributes federal funds from agencies like the EPA, NOAA, and U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service. Programs include pollinator habitat restoration, coastal resilience, and clean water initiatives. Grants range from $50,000 to $2 million.

5. U.S. Forest Service Community Forest and Open Space Program

This program provides funding to protect community forests from conversion to non-forest uses. Grants typically range from $100,000 to $400,000 and require matching funds.

NOAA Coastal Resilience Grants

Agency: National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration

Typical Award: $250,000 - $1,500,000

Summary: Funds habitat restoration projects that strengthen coastal resilience against storms, flooding, and climate change while supporting fisheries and threatened species.

Conservation Innovation Grants (CIG)

Agency: USDA Natural Resources Conservation Service

Typical Award: $50,000 - $2,000,000

Summary: Stimulates development and adoption of innovative conservation approaches and technologies. Supports projects on soil health, water quality, and sustainable land management.

Who Is Eligible?

Eligibility varies by program, but most federal environmental grants are open to:

  • 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations
  • State and local governments
  • Tribal governments
  • Universities and research institutions
  • Collaborative partnerships (often encouraged)

Many programs require or prefer partnerships between nonprofits, government agencies, and community stakeholders.

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful environmental grant applications typically focus on:

  • Habitat restoration - Wetlands, forests, streams, coastal areas
  • Water quality improvement - Stormwater management, watershed protection
  • Climate resilience - Green infrastructure, flood mitigation
  • Environmental education - K-12 programs, community workshops
  • Biodiversity conservation - Endangered species protection, invasive species removal
  • Sustainable agriculture - Soil health, pollinator habitat, regenerative practices
