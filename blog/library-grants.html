IMLS Grants for Libraries and Information Services

Public libraries, academic libraries, research libraries, and library consortia can access federal funding from the Institute of Museum and Library Services (IMLS) and other agencies. These grants support technology infrastructure, community programs, collections preservation, professional development, and innovative library services.

Library grants support everything from digitization projects to STEM programming, broadband access to library construction. Staying informed about funding opportunities helps libraries serve their communities more effectively.

GrantPing helps libraries track federal funding opportunities.

Major Federal Library Grant Programs

1. IMLS Grants to States Program

The Grants to States program provides formula funding to State Library Administrative Agencies, which then distribute funds to libraries through competitive grants. Local library awards typically range from $5,000 to $100,000 for projects like technology upgrades, programming, and professional development.

2. IMLS National Leadership Grants for Libraries

National Leadership Grants support projects that advance library practice and create models replicable nationwide. Awards range from $50,000 to $1 million for research, demonstration projects, and capacity building.

3. IMLS Native American/Native Hawaiian Library Services

This program provides grants to tribal libraries for technology, collections, and programming. Basic grants award up to $10,000; Enhancement grants range from $50,000 to $150,000.

4. IMLS Laura Bush 21st Century Librarian Program

The 21st Century Librarian program funds library education, professional development, and workforce initiatives. Awards range from $50,000 to $1 million for graduate programs, continuing education, and research.

5. NSF Advanced Technological Education (ATE) - Libraries

The National Science Foundation funds libraries partnering with community colleges on STEM technician education. Library-involved awards can range from $200,000 to $1 million.

Recent Opportunities

IMLS Grants to States - Local Library Projects

Agency: Institute of Museum and Library Services (via State Library Agencies)

Typical Award: $5,000 - $100,000

Summary: Formula grants to states for competitive distribution to libraries. Funds technology, collections, programming, early literacy, workforce development, and community engagement. Libraries apply through their State Library Administrative Agency.

IMLS National Leadership Grants - Libraries

Agency: Institute of Museum and Library Services

Typical Award: $100,000 - $1,000,000

Summary: Competitive grants for innovative projects advancing library practice. Supports digital platforms, community catalysts, collections stewardship, information policy research, and professional development initiatives that create national models.

IMLS Native American/Native Hawaiian Library Services - Enhancement Grants

Agency: Institute of Museum and Library Services

Typical Award: $50,000 - $150,000

Summary: Supports tribal libraries in expanding services, improving operations, and serving Native communities. Funds technology infrastructure, cultural programming, collections development, and library education.

Who Is Eligible?

Library grant eligibility typically includes:

  • Public libraries and library systems
  • Academic and research libraries
  • School libraries (through State Library grants)
  • Tribal libraries
  • Library consortia and cooperative organizations
  • State Library Administrative Agencies
  • Museums with library components
  • Archives and special collections

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful library grants typically support:

  • Technology infrastructure - Broadband, computers, WiFi, maker spaces
  • Community programming - STEM activities, digital literacy, job training
  • Collections development - Books, databases, streaming media
  • Digitization projects - Preserving and providing access to local history
  • Early literacy programs - Summer reading, storytimes, family engagement
  • Professional development - Training for librarians and library staff
  • Accessibility improvements - Services for people with disabilities
  • Research and innovation - New service models, data-driven practice
