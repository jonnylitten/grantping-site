DOJ and FEMA Grants for Public Safety and Emergency Services

Police departments, fire departments, emergency management agencies, and public safety organizations can access billions in federal funding from the Department of Justice (DOJ), Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA), and Department of Homeland Security (DHS). These grants support equipment purchases, training, community policing, emergency preparedness, and homeland security initiatives.

Public safety grants are highly competitive and often require matching funds. Staying informed about funding opportunities is critical to securing resources that keep communities safe.

GrantPing helps public safety agencies track federal funding opportunities.

Major Federal Public Safety Grant Programs

1. FEMA Assistance to Firefighters Grant (AFG)

The AFG program funds fire departments and EMS organizations to purchase equipment, protective gear, training, and fire prevention programs. Awards range from $10,000 to $500,000, with larger grants available for apparatus.

2. FEMA Staffing for Adequate Fire and Emergency Response (SAFER)

SAFER grants help fire departments recruit and retain firefighters. Awards can cover up to 75% of salary and benefits for new positions over three years.

3. DOJ Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant (JAG)

The JAG program is the leading federal justice grant, providing flexible funding for law enforcement, prosecution, courts, crime prevention, and corrections. Formula grants to states and localities range from $10,000 to several million dollars.

4. DOJ Community Oriented Policing Services (COPS) Grants

COPS grants advance community policing through hiring officers, purchasing equipment, and implementing innovative programs. Hiring grants can fund 75% of salary and benefits for three years; technology grants range from $50,000 to $1 million.

5. FEMA Emergency Management Performance Grant (EMPG)

EMPG provides formula funding to state and local emergency management agencies for planning, training, exercises, and operations. Awards support emergency operations centers, warning systems, and disaster preparedness.

Recent Opportunities

FEMA Assistance to Firefighters Grant (AFG)

Agency: Federal Emergency Management Agency

Typical Award: $25,000 - $500,000

Summary: Competitive grants to fire departments for operations and safety equipment, training, firefighting vehicles, emergency medical services equipment, and fire prevention and safety programs. Separate funding available for vehicle acquisition (up to $2 million).

DOJ COPS Hiring Program (CHP)

Agency: Department of Justice, Office of Community Oriented Policing Services

Typical Award: $125,000/officer for 3 years

Summary: Funds hiring and rehiring of career law enforcement officers to engage in community policing. Awards cover up to 75% of salary and benefits for entry-level positions over 36 months.

DHS State Homeland Security Program (SHSP)

Agency: Department of Homeland Security, FEMA

Typical Award: $500,000 - $50,000,000 (to states; subgrants to localities)

Summary: Formula grants supporting terrorism prevention, protection, mitigation, response, and recovery capabilities. Funds equipment, training, exercises, and planning for state and local first responders.

Who Is Eligible?

Public safety grant eligibility typically includes:

  • Fire departments and fire districts
  • Law enforcement agencies (police, sheriff)
  • Emergency medical services organizations
  • State and local emergency management agencies
  • State and local governments
  • Tribal public safety agencies
  • Nonprofit EMS organizations

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful public safety grants typically support:

  • Equipment purchases - Radios, protective gear, rescue tools, body cameras
  • Vehicles - Fire trucks, ambulances, police cruisers
  • Training - Tactical training, de-escalation, emergency response
  • Community policing - Officer hiring, community engagement programs
  • Emergency preparedness - Planning, exercises, emergency operations centers
  • Fire prevention - Smoke alarm programs, public education
  • Technology - Records management, dispatch systems, crime analysis
  • Homeland security - Terrorism prevention, cybersecurity, active shooter response
