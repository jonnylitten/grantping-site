Federal Grants for Community Health Centers and Healthcare Nonprofits

Community health centers and healthcare nonprofits provide essential services to underserved populations. Federal grants from HRSA, CDC, and other HHS agencies fund primary care expansion, health education, chronic disease prevention, and healthcare workforce development.

Major Federal Healthcare Grant Programs

1. HRSA Health Center Program (Section 330)

The Health Resources and Services Administration provides funding for Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs) and look-alike organizations. New Access Point grants typically award $650,000 to $1.5 million for the first year, with multi-year funding available.

2. CDC Preventive Health and Health Services Block Grant

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention funds state and local health departments and nonprofits for chronic disease prevention, health promotion, and emergency preparedness. Awards range from $100,000 to $5 million.

3. HRSA Rural Health Care Services Outreach Program

This program supports healthcare delivery networks in rural communities. Typical awards range from $150,000 to $400,000 per year for up to three years.

4. Maternal and Child Health Bureau Grants

HRSA's Maternal and Child Health Bureau funds programs supporting maternal health, infant care, children with special healthcare needs, and adolescent health. Awards range from $300,000 to $2 million.

5. Ryan White HIV/AIDS Program

This HRSA program provides care and treatment services for people living with HIV/AIDS. Part C grants for early intervention services typically range from $100,000 to $800,000 annually.

Recent Opportunities

HRSA Health Center Cluster Funding Opportunity

Agency: Health Resources and Services Administration

Typical Award: $650,000 - $2,000,000

Summary: Supports new health center access points and service area expansions to increase primary care access in medically underserved areas. Includes supplemental funding for capital projects.

CDC Racial and Ethnic Approaches to Community Health (REACH)

Agency: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention

Typical Award: $500,000 - $1,500,000

Summary: Funds community-based organizations working to reduce health disparities related to chronic diseases among racial and ethnic minority populations.

HRSA Nurse Corps Loan Repayment Program

Agency: Health Resources and Services Administration

Typical Award: Up to $85,000 per nurse

Summary: Provides loan repayment for registered nurses, nurse practitioners, and nurse faculty working in underserved communities. Helps healthcare nonprofits recruit and retain nursing staff.

Who Is Eligible?

Healthcare grant eligibility includes:

  • Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs) and FQHC look-alikes
  • 501(c)(3) nonprofit healthcare organizations
  • Free and charitable clinics
  • Community-based organizations with health programs
  • State and local health departments
  • Tribal health organizations
  • Rural health clinics

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful healthcare grants typically support:

  • Primary care access expansion - New clinic sites, mobile health units
  • Chronic disease prevention - Diabetes, heart disease, obesity programs
  • Maternal and child health - Prenatal care, well-child visits, immunizations
  • Mental health and substance use services - Integrated behavioral health
  • Workforce recruitment and training - Loan repayment, residency programs
  • Health equity initiatives - Reducing disparities in underserved populations
  • Telehealth infrastructure - Remote patient monitoring, virtual care
