Federal Grants for Youth Development Programs

Youth development organizations—from afterschool programs to mentoring initiatives, job training to juvenile justice diversion—can access substantial federal funding from the Department of Justice, Administration for Children and Families, Department of Labor, and other agencies.

These grants support programs that keep young people safe, engaged in learning, and on pathways to success. Knowing when funding opportunities are announced is critical to sustaining and expanding youth services.

GrantPing helps youth-serving organizations track federal funding opportunities.

Major Federal Youth Development Grant Programs

1. 21st Century Community Learning Centers (21st CCLC)

The Department of Education's 21st CCLC program provides formula grants to states, which then compete local awards for afterschool and summer enrichment programs. Awards typically range from $50,000 to $400,000 per year over five years.

2. DOJ Youth Mentoring Grants

The Office of Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention (OJJDP) funds mentoring programs for at-risk youth. Awards range from $300,000 to $750,000 over three years.

3. ACF Runaway and Homeless Youth (RHY) Programs

The Administration for Children and Families funds basic centers, transitional living programs, and street outreach for runaway and homeless youth. Awards range from $100,000 to $400,000.

4. DOL YouthBuild Program

The Department of Labor funds YouthBuild programs providing education, occupational training, and construction skills for low-income youth. Awards typically range from $700,000 to $1.1 million.

5. Corporation for National and Community Service AmeriCorps Grants

AmeriCorps funds programs engaging young adults in service while providing education awards. Awards range from $100,000 to over $1 million for youth-focused programs.

Recent Opportunities

OJJDP Mentoring Opportunities for Youth Initiative

Agency: Office of Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention

Typical Award: $400,000 - $750,000

Summary: Supports evidence-based mentoring programs for youth at risk of involvement in the juvenile justice system, including group mentoring, one-on-one mentoring, and peer mentoring models.

ACF Personal Responsibility Education Program (PREP)

Agency: Administration for Children and Families

Typical Award: $200,000 - $800,000

Summary: Funds evidence-based programs educating adolescents on healthy relationships, pregnancy prevention, and adulthood preparation. Targets youth in foster care, homeless youth, and those living in high teen birth rate areas.

DOL Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) Youth Programs

Agency: Department of Labor

Typical Award: $300,000 - $2,000,000

Summary: Formula grants to states for youth workforce development services including job training, education, career counseling, and supportive services for in-school and out-of-school youth ages 14-24.

Who Is Eligible?

Youth program grant eligibility varies by program but typically includes:

  • 501(c)(3) nonprofit youth-serving organizations
  • School districts and educational institutions
  • Community-based organizations
  • Faith-based organizations
  • State and local government agencies
  • Tribal organizations
  • Workforce development boards

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful youth development grants typically support:

  • Afterschool and summer programs - Academic enrichment, recreation, STEM activities
  • Mentoring programs - One-on-one, group, peer mentoring models
  • Job training and workforce development - Skills training, internships, career pathways
  • Juvenile justice diversion - Alternatives to incarceration, reentry programs
  • Youth leadership development - Civic engagement, service learning
  • Services for vulnerable youth - Foster care, homeless, justice-involved youth
  • Prevention programs - Substance abuse, violence, pregnancy prevention
