HUD Grants for Homeless Services and Shelters

Organizations providing homeless services—emergency shelters, transitional housing, permanent supportive housing, and case management—rely on federal funding from the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) and other agencies.

HUD's Continuum of Care (CoC) program alone distributes over $3 billion annually to homeless service providers. Missing application deadlines or not understanding funding priorities can mean the difference between securing housing for families and turning them away.

Major Federal Homeless Services Grant Programs

1. HUD Continuum of Care (CoC) Program

The Continuum of Care Program is the largest federal homeless assistance program. It funds permanent supportive housing, rapid rehousing, transitional housing, and supportive services. Annual awards range from $100,000 to several million dollars for established providers.

2. Emergency Solutions Grants (ESG)

ESG funds emergency shelter operations, rapid rehousing, homelessness prevention, and outreach services. Formula grants go to states and cities, which then distribute funding to local nonprofits. Typical awards range from $50,000 to $500,000.

3. HUD Youth Homelessness Demonstration Program (YHDP)

This program supports communities developing coordinated responses to youth homelessness. Awards range from $1 million to $10 million for comprehensive system planning and implementation.

4. HUD Housing Opportunities for Persons with AIDS (HOPWA)

HOPWA provides housing assistance and supportive services for people living with HIV/AIDS. Formula and competitive grants range from $100,000 to $2 million.

5. VA Supportive Services for Veteran Families (SSVF)

The Department of Veterans Affairs funds rapid rehousing and homelessness prevention for veteran families. Awards typically range from $200,000 to $1.5 million.

Agency: U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development

Typical Award: $150,000 - $5,000,000

Summary: Annual competition funding permanent supportive housing for chronically homeless individuals and families, rapid rehousing programs, and dedicated HMIS (Homeless Management Information System) funding.

Emergency Solutions Grants Program

Agency: U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development

Typical Award: $75,000 - $500,000

Summary: Provides funding for emergency shelter operations, street outreach, rapid rehousing assistance, and homelessness prevention services. Administered through state and local governments.

VA Supportive Services for Veteran Families (SSVF)

Agency: Department of Veterans Affairs

Typical Award: $300,000 - $1,500,000

Summary: Funds nonprofit organizations providing case management, short-term financial assistance, and housing stabilization services to very low-income veteran families at risk of homelessness.

Who Is Eligible?

Homeless services grant eligibility includes:

  • 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations
  • State and local governments
  • Public housing agencies
  • Continuum of Care collaborative applicants
  • Faith-based organizations
  • Tribal organizations

Most HUD homeless programs require participation in the local Continuum of Care and HMIS data reporting.

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful homeless services grants typically support:

  • Permanent supportive housing - Long-term housing with case management
  • Rapid rehousing - Short-term rental assistance and services
  • Emergency shelter - Operations, essential services, case management
  • Homelessness prevention - Financial assistance, mediation, case management
  • Street outreach - Engagement of unsheltered individuals
  • HMIS systems - Data collection and reporting infrastructure
  • Coordinated entry - Centralized assessment and referral systems
