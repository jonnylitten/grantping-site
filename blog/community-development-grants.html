CDBG and Community Development Grants

Community development organizations, municipalities, and nonprofits can access federal funding for neighborhood revitalization, infrastructure improvements, economic development, and services for low- and moderate-income residents through Community Development Block Grants (CDBG) and other programs.

CDBG alone distributes over $3 billion annually to communities across America. Additional funding comes from the Economic Development Administration, HUD's Choice Neighborhoods, and other federal initiatives.

GrantPing helps community development organizations track these funding opportunities.

Major Federal Community Development Grant Programs

1. HUD Community Development Block Grant (CDBG)

The CDBG program is the largest federal community development program, providing formula grants to entitlement communities and states. Competitive CDBG grants are also available for specific initiatives, ranging from $500,000 to $5 million.

2. EDA Public Works and Economic Adjustment Assistance

The Economic Development Administration funds infrastructure projects that support economic development, job creation, and regional competitiveness. Awards typically range from $500,000 to $10 million.

3. HUD Choice Neighborhoods Initiative

Choice Neighborhoods transforms distressed public and assisted housing into mixed-income communities. Planning grants award up to $500,000; implementation grants range from $20 million to $50 million.

4. HUD Section 108 Loan Guarantee Program

This program allows CDBG entitlement communities to borrow against future CDBG allocations for large-scale economic development and infrastructure projects. Loans can reach $35 million or more.

5. USDA Community Facilities Direct Loan and Grant Program

USDA Rural Development funds essential community facilities in rural areas. Grants cover up to 75% of project costs, with awards ranging from $50,000 to $2 million.

Recent Opportunities

EDA Build Back Better Regional Challenge

Agency: Economic Development Administration

Typical Award: $25,000,000 - $100,000,000

Summary: Supports transformative regional economic development projects that strengthen supply chains, grow industry clusters, create quality jobs, and build inclusive prosperity across communities.

HUD Community Development Block Grant (CDBG-DR) Disaster Recovery

Agency: U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development

Typical Award: $1,000,000 - $500,000,000+

Summary: Provides flexible funding for disaster recovery, long-term rebuilding, infrastructure restoration, and housing rehabilitation in communities affected by presidentially-declared disasters.

HUD Community Project Funding (Congressionally Directed Spending)

Agency: U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development

Typical Award: $500,000 - $5,000,000

Summary: Supports locally-prioritized projects including affordable housing, community facilities, infrastructure, and economic development initiatives through congressional earmarks.

Who Is Eligible?

Community development grant eligibility varies but typically includes:

  • Entitlement cities and urban counties (for CDBG formula grants)
  • State governments
  • 501(c)(3) nonprofit community development organizations
  • Community Development Corporations (CDCs)
  • Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFIs)
  • Regional planning organizations
  • Economic development districts

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful community development grants typically support:

  • Infrastructure improvements - Streets, water/sewer, broadband, public facilities
  • Housing rehabilitation - Home repair programs, blight removal, accessibility modifications
  • Economic development - Business districts, job creation, commercial revitalization
  • Public facilities - Community centers, parks, libraries, health clinics
  • Public services - Job training, childcare, senior services, youth programs
  • Neighborhood planning - Comprehensive community development strategies
  • Disaster recovery - Long-term rebuilding after floods, hurricanes, wildfires
