Federal Grants for Tribal Governments and Native American Programs

Tribal governments, Alaska Native villages, Native Hawaiian organizations, and nonprofits serving Native communities can access federal funding from the Bureau of Indian Affairs (BIA), Indian Health Service (IHS), HUD, DOJ, and dozens of other agencies. These grants support healthcare, housing, education, economic development, infrastructure, and cultural preservation.

Federal funding for tribes comes through direct appropriations, formula grants, and competitive programs. Staying informed about funding opportunities across all agencies is essential to supporting tribal sovereignty and community wellbeing.

Major Federal Tribal Grant Programs

1. IHS Indian Health Service Grants

The Indian Health Service provides formula and competitive grants for tribal healthcare facilities, clinical services, substance abuse treatment, and health promotion. Awards range from $100,000 to over $10 million for comprehensive healthcare programs.

2. HUD Indian Housing Block Grant (IHBG)

The IHBG provides formula funding to tribal housing authorities for affordable housing development, renovation, and housing services. Awards range from $50,000 to $10 million annually based on need and population.

3. BIA Tribal Transportation Program

The Bureau of Indian Affairs funds road construction, maintenance, and transportation planning on tribal lands. Formula and competitive awards range from $100,000 to $20 million.

4. DOJ Coordinated Tribal Assistance Solicitation (CTAS)

CTAS consolidates tribal justice and public safety grants into one application. Awards range from $100,000 to $2 million for law enforcement, courts, corrections, and victim services.

5. ACF Native American Programs

The Administration for Children and Families funds social and economic development projects including language preservation, environmental protection, and community development. Awards range from $100,000 to $600,000.

Recent Opportunities

IHS Sanitation Facilities Construction Program

Agency: Indian Health Service

Typical Award: $500,000 - $5,000,000

Summary: Funds water supply, sewage disposal, and solid waste systems in tribal communities. Supports construction of essential sanitation infrastructure to eliminate disease and improve health conditions on tribal lands.

DOJ CTAS - Tribal Justice Support

Agency: Department of Justice

Typical Award: $250,000 - $2,000,000

Summary: Consolidated tribal justice grants supporting law enforcement, tribal courts, corrections, victim services, substance abuse treatment, and youth programs. Single application covers multiple program areas addressing public safety in tribal communities.

BIA Tiwahe Initiative - Strong Families

Agency: Bureau of Indian Affairs

Typical Award: $500,000 - $2,500,000

Summary: Supports comprehensive family services including behavioral health, child welfare, education, and economic development. Uses coordinated approach to strengthen tribal families and reduce dependency on multiple fragmented programs.

Who Is Eligible?

Tribal grant eligibility typically includes:

  • Federally recognized Indian tribes
  • Alaska Native villages and corporations
  • Native Hawaiian organizations
  • Tribal organizations and consortia
  • Tribal housing authorities
  • Tribal colleges and universities
  • Urban Indian health organizations
  • Nonprofits serving Native communities

Most BIA and IHS programs require federal recognition; some programs serve all Native communities regardless of recognition status.

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful tribal grants typically support:

  • Healthcare services - Clinics, hospitals, behavioral health, substance abuse treatment
  • Housing development - New construction, rehabilitation, rental assistance
  • Infrastructure - Roads, water systems, broadband, energy
  • Education programs - Schools, early childhood, higher education scholarships
  • Economic development - Business development, tourism, workforce training
  • Public safety - Law enforcement, tribal courts, victim services
  • Cultural preservation - Language revitalization, historic preservation, traditional practices
  • Environmental protection - Natural resource management, climate adaptation
