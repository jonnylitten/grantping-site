Federal Grants for Early Childhood Education and Head Start

Early childhood programs, Head Start grantees, childcare centers, and preschools can access billions in federal funding from the Administration for Children and Families (ACF), Department of Education, and other agencies. These grants support high-quality early learning, comprehensive child development, and childcare for working families.

Early childhood grants support the most critical years of child development. Staying informed about funding opportunities ensures your program can serve more children and families.

GrantPing helps early childhood organizations track federal funding opportunities.

Major Federal Early Childhood Grant Programs

1. ACF Head Start and Early Head Start

The Head Start program provides comprehensive early childhood services to low-income children and families. Grants support education, health, nutrition, and family support services. Awards range from $500,000 to over $10 million depending on program size and enrollment.

2. ACF Child Care and Development Fund (CCDF)

The CCDF provides formula grants to states for childcare subsidies and quality improvement. States distribute funds to childcare providers and quality initiatives. Quality improvement grants support professional development, curriculum, and facility improvements.

3. Department of Education Preschool Development Grants (PDG)

PDG grants help states build or expand high-quality preschool programs. Awards range from $5 million to $20 million per state for mixed-delivery preschool systems serving low- and moderate-income children.

4. ACF Maternal, Infant, and Early Childhood Home Visiting (MIECHV)

The MIECHV program funds evidence-based home visiting programs for pregnant women and families with young children. Formula grants to states range from $500,000 to $20 million annually.

5. ACF Early Head Start Child Care Partnerships

EHS-CCP grants support partnerships between Early Head Start grantees and childcare providers to deliver comprehensive services in community-based childcare settings. Awards range from $500,000 to $3 million.

Recent Opportunities

ACF Head Start Program - New and Expansion Grants

Agency: Administration for Children and Families

Typical Award: $750,000 - $12,000,000

Summary: Competitive grants for new Head Start and Early Head Start programs or expansion of existing programs. Comprehensive services include early learning, health screenings, nutrition, mental health, and family support for children birth to age 5 and pregnant women.

ACF Child Care and Development Fund (CCDF) Quality Improvement

Agency: Administration for Children and Families

Typical Award: $50,000 - $500,000 (via state agencies)

Summary: States use CCDF quality funds to improve childcare through professional development, quality rating systems, curriculum development, facility improvements, and infant-toddler care expansion. Providers apply through state childcare agencies.

Department of Education Preschool Development Grant Birth Through Five (PDG B-5)

Agency: U.S. Department of Education

Typical Award: $5,000,000 - $15,000,000

Summary: Supports states in developing and expanding high-quality preschool programs, particularly for children from low-income families. Funds needs assessments, strategic planning, and expansion of mixed-delivery systems including public schools, Head Start, and community providers.

Who Is Eligible?

Early childhood grant eligibility typically includes:

  • 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations
  • Head Start and Early Head Start grantees
  • Childcare centers and family childcare providers
  • School districts operating preschool programs
  • State and local governments
  • Tribal organizations
  • Faith-based organizations

What Types of Projects Get Funded?

Successful early childhood grants typically support:

  • Comprehensive early learning - Education, health, nutrition, family services (Head Start model)
  • Infant-toddler care - Early Head Start, childcare for children under 3
  • Preschool programs - State pre-K, school district preschools
  • Quality improvement - Professional development, curriculum, assessments
  • Childcare subsidies - Vouchers for low-income working families
  • Facility improvements - Playground equipment, classroom materials, safety upgrades
  • Home visiting - Evidence-based programs for pregnant women and families
  • Family engagement - Parent education, family literacy, support services
